## The Artist Side: How People Actually Make Money Here

Let's get real about earnings. Redbubble introduced a tiered fee structure in 2025, and it's been... let's say "controversial" is putting it mildly.

**The Three Tiers:**

**Standard Tier:** New and casual artists start here. Platform takes 50% of monthly earnings as a fee. Yeah, you read that right â€“ 50%. But wait, there's a cap of $150 per payment period, which protects artists who are doing well.

**Premium Tier:** More established artists land here. Platform fee drops to 20% of monthly earnings. Still capped at $150 max.

**Pro Tier:** The dream tier. High-performing artists who consistently sell and engage with the platform. No platform fees. No excess markup fees. Just pure profit margin.

There's also this thing called the "excess markup fee" â€“ if you price things more than 20% above base price, Redbubble takes an additional 50% of anything earned above that 20% threshold. It's their way of keeping prices competitive, but it also means you can't just slap a 200% markup on everything and call it a day.
